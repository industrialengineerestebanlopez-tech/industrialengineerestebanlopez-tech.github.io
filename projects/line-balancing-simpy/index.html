Line balancing of a packaging line

Most shop-floor work happens on site — so here I show the method on a digital twin. A 6-station packaging line is modelled in SimPy to find the bottleneck, compare it against takt time, and quantify the gain from balancing the line in throughput, lead time and WIP.

DEFINE The problem

A packaging/conditioning line must deliver 80 units/hour, so its takt time is 45 s/unit. If any single station needs longer than takt, it throttles the whole line and demand is missed. The question: where is the bottleneck, and how much do we gain by rebalancing — without touching the physical line first?

MEASURE · ANALYZE Bottleneck vs takt

Each station's cycle time against takt. Any bar above the dashed line is a station that cannot keep up with demand — here, station S3.

Finding: S3 needs 55 s but takt is 45 s → utilisation 1.22 (>1). A single overloaded station caps the line at ~65 u/h, below the 80 u/h demand. Rebalancing ~13 s of S3's work to lighter stations (same total work content) puts every station under takt.

CONTROL Sustain the balance

  • Standardised work at each station so cycle times stay near the balanced target.
  • Visual takt on the line; react when a station drifts above it (the new bottleneck).
  • Re-balance when demand changes — takt is set by demand, so recompute when the order rate moves.
  • Attack variability, not just the mean. The simulation shows high variability inflates WIP and lead time even when means fit under takt.
  • PDCA by simulation. Test every proposed change on the model before touching the physical line.
