PROBLEM The brief

Design a manufacturable mechanical power-transmission product around a selected spur pinion. The pinion is chosen from a catalogue, scaled to a target size, modelled with a real shaft and keyway, joined to an adapter disc and a main disc, and fully documented with manufacturing drawings and an assembly — exactly the workflow of a junior mechanical/manufacturing engineer.

METHOD How I built it — engineering decisions

1
Pinion selection. Module m = 8 and Z = 30 teeth fixed by the brief; selected reference A1-399-30-1 from the TraceParts catalogue (rated torque 1834 N·m).
2
Proportional scaling. The brief's outer diameter (46 mm) vs. the catalogue's 256 mm gives a scale factor of ≈ 5.6×; every part is scaled to keep the assembly consistent.
3
Pinion modelling. Removed the old shaft, filled the bore, extended the pinion, extruded the shaft on both sides and added an angled section and chamfers — a documented, ordered feature tree (below).
4
Key (chaveta) design. Rectangular form B, sized to ≈ ¼ of the shaft diameter per UNI 6604-69 / DIN 6885, selected from the Rodavigo catalogue → 89.6 × 40 × 22 mm.
5
Discs & joints. Adapter disc + main disc, joined with 3 × DIN 912 M27 bolts and 2 × DIN EN ISO 2338 Ø30 h8 dowel pins.
6
Material & drawings. AISI 316 stainless (E ≈ 193 GPa, Rm = 590 MPa, ρ = 8000 kg/m³); full GD&T manufacturing drawings with sections, detail views and a normalized title block, plus an assembly with BOM and exploded animation.

CONCLUSION What it shows

This is a complete, manufacturable mechanical product taken from a brief to production-ready documentation.
