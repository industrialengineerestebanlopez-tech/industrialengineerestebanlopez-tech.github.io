DEFINE The problem

Unplanned breakdowns are the enemy of availability — the first pillar of OEE. Reactive maintenance means stopping the line when it is most expensive. The goal: predict failures early enough to convert unplanned stops into planned interventions (the heart of TPM).

MEASURE · ANALYZE Which failure mode first?

A Pareto of the five failure modes shows where reliability effort pays off. The donut shows the core modelling challenge: only 3.4% of cycles fail.

Finding: Heat-dissipation (HDF, 115), Overstrain (OSF, 98) and Power (PWF, 95) failures account for 82.6% of all failure occurrences. They share one physical root — torque, rotational speed and the air–process temperature gap — so those physics become the model's features.

ANALYZE Physics of failure (features)

Instead of feeding raw sensors blindly, I derive the quantities that actually cause the vital-few modes:

Mechanical power = Torque × angular speed → power failures Temperature gap = Process − Air → heat-dissipation failures Tool wear × Torque → overstrain failures

IMPROVE Failure-prediction model

A Random Forest predicts the Machine failure label from the process variables and the engineered physics.

Engineering judgement: with only 3.4% failures, accuracy is misleading — a model that predicts “never fails” already scores ~96.6%. The metric that matters for maintenance is recall on the failure class: the share of real breakdowns caught in time.

CONTROL From prediction to avoided downtime

  • Predict, don't react. Each flagged failure turns an unplanned stop into a planned one — a direct lift to availability and OEE.
  • Prioritise the vital-few modes. Set SPC limits on temperature gap, torque and mechanical power (HDF, OSF, PWF).
  • Tune the threshold, not just the model. Trade false alarms for higher recall based on the cost of a breakdown vs. a check.
  • Condition-based alarms. Turn the top features into simple shop-floor thresholds as a poka-yoke backup.
  • PDCA. Track MTBF and recall monthly; retrain as tooling and the machine change.
