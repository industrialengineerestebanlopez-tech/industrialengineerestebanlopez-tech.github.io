Defect reduction in a steel rolling line

A continuous-improvement case study on real inspection data from 1,941 steel plates: defect prioritisation with a Pareto chart, statistical process control (SPC / Cpk) and an inspection-support classifier. Framework: the DMAIC cycle.

DEFINE The problem

In steel rolling, surface defects drive rework, scrap and customer claims. Without a data-based priority, improvement effort scatters across many failure modes with very different impact. Goal: find where to act first to remove the largest share of defects with the least effort.

MEASURE · ANALYZE Pareto chart

The 80/20 rule separates the “vital few” from the “trivial many”. The cyan bars accumulate ~80% of all defects — that is where improvement must focus.

Finding: Other_Faults (673), Bumps (402) and K_Scatch (391) account for 75.5% of all defective plates. Moreover, the fact that the largest category is “Other_Faults” (a catch-all bucket) reveals an opportunity to improve the defect taxonomy and traceability — a management finding, not only a process one.

CONTROL Countermeasures & sustainability

  • Prioritise the vital few. Tackle Other_Faults, Bumps and K_Scatch first: ~75% of the problem at the lowest effort.
  • Redefine the defect taxonomy. Shrink the “Other_Faults” catch-all to enable fine root-cause analysis.
  • SPC on critical variables. Monitor the model's highest-importance variables with control charts.
  • Digital poka-yoke. Deploy the classifier as inspection support to standardise the criterion.
  • PDCA loop. Recompute the Pareto and retrain per shift/batch to verify the effect of countermeasures.
